Cross-Sell Playbook: 8 Initiatives to Convert the Base

Every SOHO and Small Business account maps to one of eight profiles based on what it holds and bills today. This playbook turns each of the eight BI-defined groups into an activation strategy: an overview, the target offer, and a phased outreach motion — digital, automated, human, and sustained — with a measurement plan and a responder-exclusion mechanism at every phase.

How to read each initiative: the left card is the account's profile today, the right card is the target offer we lead with. Below that, every initiative runs the same four-phase motion, tailored to the offer and the segment — Phase 1 is a self-service digital touch that creates a Salesforce Lead, Phase 2 is an automated dialer sweep of non-responders, Phase 3 is a manual, human touch from the field or account teams, and Phase 4 closes the loop with re-engagement and reporting. Every phase excludes accounts that already responded or converted in a prior phase, tracked as a Customer Interaction / Lead Status on the account in Salesforce.

Program timeline

Phase 1 sends the initial touch on Day 0, one reminder on Day 5, and closes on Day 10 — non-responders move into Phase 2 immediately after. Phase 4 stays open-ended, since it's the standing nurture and reporting motion rather than a one-time push.

Strategy overview — This is a low-bill, mobile-only base with no fixed anchor on the account, so the play is to establish a home/office internet connection at a flat, easy-to-say price point. Because current spend is light ($37/mo), the messaging leads with value and simplicity — "one line today, home internet for $84.99" — rather than a bundle discount, since there isn't yet a second service to bundle against.

Phased activation plan

1
Digital warm-up — email + self-service form
Owner: Marketing / CX
Tools: Email platform (Emarsys), SMS gateway, WhatsApp (pending confirmation as a channel), Vercel-hosted landing page/form, Salesforce Fixed (Broadband) (Lead + Campaign object), Customer Service IVR/queue for inbound calls.
  1. Send a bilingual email/SMS to the 1,815 accounts: "Add 1GB Internet for $84.99/mo" with a link to a landing page.
  2. Landing page captures name, account/mobile number, best time to call, and consent — submission creates a Salesforce Lead tagged Campaign = SOHO-Mobile-Under60-FixedAttach, auto-routed to a queue of assigned agents.
  3. Include the customer service number on every touchpoint so an interested customer can call in directly without waiting for a callback.
  4. Agent SLA: call back submitted leads within 24 business hours.
  5. Send the initial email/SMS on Day 0; if there's no response, send one reminder on Day 5. The phase closes on Day 10 — any account still unresponsive at that point moves to Phase 2.
Measurement
  • Delivery / open / click-through rate
  • Landing page completion rate
  • Leads created in SF
  • Agent contact & close rate on leads
  • Cost per lead / cost per sale
2
Automated dialer sweep of non-responders
Owner: Telesales / Dialer ops
Tools: Progressive Dialer (outbound mode confirmed for this strategy) — specific platform to confirm with IVR Team (e.g. Aspect, Genesys, Cisco or other), Salesforce Fixed (Broadband) (Campaign Member status, Customer Interaction logging), call recording/QA tool, and a script provided by the Learning Experience Team.
  1. Export the 1,815-account base to Salesforce as a Campaign; filter out anyone with a Phase 1 Lead or Customer Interaction of type "Responded/Converted."
  2. Upload the remaining base to the outbound dialer with the same $84.99 1GB offer script.
  3. Customer presses # to accept and is transferred live to an available agent; otherwise the call logs as no-interest or no-answer.
  4. Log every dial outcome back to Salesforce as a Customer Interaction against the account.
Measurement
  • Dial attempts vs. connects (contact rate)
  • % pressing # (interest rate)
  • Agent-transfer conversion rate
  • Sales closed / cost per acquisition
Exclusion mechanism: Lead Status ("Responded"/"Converted") or Customer Interaction log from Phase 1 filters the dialer file before upload.
3
Human touch — field & account teams
Owner: D2D / Retail / Account Executives
Tools: Salesforce Fixed (Broadband) (order entry & Customer Interaction logging), D2D route/territory management tool, tablet-based point-of-sale app, and a script provided by the Learning Experience Team.
  1. Remaining non-responders (Phase 1 + Phase 2) route to Door-to-Door reps for the residential/SOHO footprint, since this segment behaves more like a home-based account.
  2. Reps carry a target list by zone with the offer pre-loaded and a simple close path (tablet-based order entry).
  3. Every visit/attempt logs as a Customer Interaction, whether or not it converts.
Measurement
  • Accounts assigned vs. visited
  • Contact & close rate
  • Revenue realized vs. $0.09M target
4
Sustain & learn — nurture and closed-loop reporting
Owner: Marketing Ops / RevOps
Tools: Salesforce Fixed (Broadband) reporting/dashboards, billing platform (bill insert), SMS gateway, Salesforce Fixed (Broadband) account banner/Chatter, BI dashboard (funnel tracking across phases).
  1. Accounts untouched by Phases 1–3 roll into a quarterly evergreen nurture (bill insert / SMS / Salesforce account banner) rather than being dropped.
  2. Capture close/loss reason on every attempted sale to refine offer or price in the next cycle.
  3. Publish a weekly funnel dashboard: sent → leads → dials → visits → sold, by phase.
Measurement
  • Cumulative funnel conversion vs. 5% target
  • ARPU lift realized at 90 days post-sale
  • Attach retention (churn of the new internet line)
